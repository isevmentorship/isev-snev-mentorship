Professional development mentorship tailored to the EV field.

The ISEV–SNEV Mentorship Program pairs extracellular vesicle researchers one-to-one for a 12-month professional-development relationship — matched on career topics, focus area, time zone, and working style, with identities kept private until both sides agree to proceed.

This is a career-development program, not technical research consulting. Pairs focus on things like grant-writing, career transitions, leadership, communication, and navigating the EV community — not on troubleshooting a specific assay.

About the program

A human-first alternative to speed-networking.

Early- and mid-career researchers often know what they want to work on — grant-writing, navigating a postdoc-to-PI transition, moving between academia and industry, building a scientific network, or sharpening communication and leadership — but struggle to find a mentor whose experience, schedule, and temperament actually align. The ISEV–SNEV Mentorship Program closes that gap.

Applications are reviewed by a small committee drawn from ISEV and SNEV. Once accepted, each participant receives one to three blinded candidate matches generated from their application. When both parties agree, the committee re-confirms the match, identities are revealed, and both sides sign a short program compact that sets expectations for the 12-month relationship.

How it works

Five steps, no awkward intros.

Apply

Tell us your role (mentor or mentee), career stage, EV focus area, ranked career topics, time zone, and availability. Takes about 8 minutes.

Review

A joint ISEV–SNEV committee reviews your application for fit with the program and confirms your eligibility.

See blinded matches

You receive 1–3 anonymized candidate profiles — career topics, EV focus, time zone, and fit score, with names and affiliations redacted.

Choose & sign

If both sides pick each other, the committee re-confirms the pairing, identities are unblinded, and both parties digitally sign the Mentorship Program Compact.

Meet for 12 months

Pairs meet bi-monthly for a year, supported by role-specific toolkits and a mid-year check-in. A closeout survey informs the next cycle.

For mentees

Who should apply as a mentee?

  • Graduate students, postdocs, and early-career researchers working in, or moving into, EV biology who want structured career development over a year.
  • Clinician-scientists and industry scientists looking for an external perspective on a career transition.
  • Mentees should bring a concrete set of career topics they want to work on — not a specific experiment they want debugged.

SNEV membership is encouraged but not required. ISEV membership is not required to apply.

For mentors

Who should apply as a mentor?

  • PIs, senior postdocs, staff scientists, and senior industry researchers willing to meet with each mentee bi-monthly (every other month) for 12 months — roughly one hour per meeting.
  • Mentors choose to support 1, 2, or 3 mentees per cycle.
  • Mentors from underrepresented regions and backgrounds are especially encouraged to apply.
  • Prior mentoring experience is welcomed but not required — we provide a short onboarding and a mentor toolkit.

Active ISEV membership is preferred for mentors.

Frequently asked

Questions we hear a lot.

Why keep things blinded at first?
Blinding reduces the influence of prestige, nationality, and prior acquaintance on initial match selection. It keeps the focus on training goals and working-style fit. Once both parties mutually choose, identities are revealed before any meeting.
What happens if I don't find a match I like?
You can decline all of your blinded matches. Your application returns to the pool and the committee will generate a new set of candidates in the next matching cycle.
What's the expected time commitment?
A typical pairing meets bi-monthly (every other month) for twelve months — roughly one hour per meeting, plus light asynchronous communication. That's six meetings over the year. Pairs are free to continue informally afterwards.
Is this program for technical research advice?
No. This is a professional-development program. Pairs focus on career topics — grant-writing, transitions, leadership, communication, networking, publishing strategy, and so on. We ask mentors and mentees to steer away from discussing the specifics of the mentee's current research project, both to protect intellectual property and to keep the relationship focused on career growth rather than ad-hoc consulting.
Is there a cost?
No. Participation is free for both mentors and mentees, thanks to ISEV and SNEV support.
How many mentees can a mentor support?
Mentors choose their own support level when applying: 1, 2, or 3 mentees per 12-month cycle. Matches are drawn from a rolling pool, so mentors may be matched with their mentees at different times across the year.
Who runs the program?
The program is operated jointly by the International Society for Extracellular Vesicles (ISEV) and the Student Network for EVs (SNEV), with a volunteer matching committee drawn from both organizations.
How do I reach the committee with a question?
Email isevmentorship@gmail.com. We try to answer within a few working days.
